◉ Latent Space z

A random noise vector sampled from a simple prior distribution (typically Gaussian). This is the only input to the Generator — it provides the stochastic variation that lets G produce diverse outputs from a fixed-dimension seed.

z ~ N(0, I), z ∈ ℝ¹⁰⁰
Each sample → unique generated output

⬡ Generator G(z)

A neural network that maps low-dimensional noise z to the data space. It learns a mapping from the latent distribution to the real data distribution, progressively expanding dimensions through fully-connected layers with BatchNorm and ReLU activations.

G : ℝ¹⁰⁰ → ℝ⁷⁸⁴
Goal: p_g ≈ p_data

◈ Discriminator D(x)

A binary classifier that receives either a real sample x or a generated sample G(z) and outputs a single scalar — the probability that the input came from real data rather than from the Generator.

D : ℝ⁷⁸⁴ → [0, 1]
1 = real data, 0 = generated fake

▲ Minimax Objective

The adversarial game: D tries to maximize its classification accuracy while G tries to minimize it. At Nash equilibrium, G produces perfect fakes and D outputs 0.5 for all inputs (can't distinguish real from fake).

V(D,G) = E_x[log D(x)] + E_z[log(1 − D(G(z)))]
Optimal: p_g = p_data, D(x) = 0.5 ∀x

▸ Training Loop (Alternating Optimization)

Step 1 — Train Discriminator

Feed a batch of real samples (label=1) and a batch of fake samples from G (label=0). Update D's weights via gradient ascent to maximize its ability to tell them apart. Often trained for k steps per G step.

θ_D ← θ_D + α · ∇[log D(x) + log(1−D(G(z)))]
Step 2 — Train Generator

Generate fake samples and pass them through (frozen) D. Update G's weights via gradient descent to fool D — making D output values closer to 1 for fakes. In practice, maximize log D(G(z)) instead for better gradients.

θ_G ← θ_G − α · ∇[log(1 − D(G(z)))]
Practical: maximize log D(G(z))
Convergence

Repeat Steps 1-2 for thousands of iterations. At convergence, G captures the full data distribution and D can no longer distinguish real from generated — outputting 0.5 everywhere. Training is notoriously unstable in vanilla GANs.

p_g → p_data
D(x) → 0.5 ∀x (Nash Equilibrium)
